Project Details

Using Sentiment Analysis through Natural Language Processing (NLP) to Analyze and Predict Stock Market Trends. Predicting Stock Market Trends through Sentiment Analysis using Natural Language Processing (NLP): A Comparative Study of Machine Learning Algorithms.

In order to proceed with this objective, we needed to understand what Sentimental Analysis is. Sentimental Analysis is an analytical method that the computer uses to understand a natural language and deduce if the message is positive, neutral or negative. In our case, Sentimental analysis refers to the deduction of the news headlines if they increase the stock or reduce it. By doing so, we end up with the ‘emotional’ status of the data which is what sentimental analysis gives its user.

With the advent of natural language processing (NLP) and sentiment analysis, it is now possible to predict stock market trends by analyzing public opinion. Sentiment analysis, a subfield of NLP, can be used to quickly understand if the tone of a report is positive, negative, or litigious, etc. [1]. Analysis of sentiment from news articles and social media posts can provide valuable insights for investors [2]. However, the accuracy of sentiment analysis can be affected by external influences, and NLP algorithms can struggle to accurately identify the sentiment of a piece of text, particularly if the text is sarcastic or ironic [2]. Nevertheless, various state-of-the-art NLP models, including BERT-based models, are designed, implemented, and tested to predict stock price movements. Mehtab et al. (2019) compared several machine learning and deep learning-based LSTM models to predict the NIFTY 50 index values of the National Stock Exchange of India [3]. Wang and Wang (2016) used data from Sina Weibo and the SVM algorithm for stock price prediction, concluding that sentiment from social media contributed to improving prediction results [3]. Ji et al. (2021) used investors' comments and companies' news of the top 15 listed medical companies from the “Oriental Fortune website” to build long text feature vectors [3]. Kameshwari et al. (2021) used sentiment analysis of news headlines from Reddit in addition to DJIA prices to forecast stock market movement using various machine learning algorithms [3]. The goal of this research is to use textual data as an additional resource of information about the stock market in making predictions [3]. The dataset used in this study contains Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) prices and Wall Street Journal news headlines from January 2008 to December 2020, and conventional statistical models for technical analysis are implemented as a benchmark [3]. The best performing model used a fine-tuned version of BERT called FinBERT for sentiment analysis of financial text, and the sentiment extracted from news headlines was fed into the GRU cell along with historical prices to achieve the best result [3]. Overall, using news headlines alongside historical prices improves stock price predictions [3].
